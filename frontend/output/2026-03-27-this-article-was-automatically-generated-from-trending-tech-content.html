Why So Many Control Rooms Were Seafoam Green

Published: 2026-03-27 • TechPulse
history
design
technology

If you've ever seen photos of mission control centers or old server rooms, you might have noticed a distinctive color scheme: seafoam green everywhere.

The Color Choice

Seafoam green wasn't just an aesthetic choice - it was carefully selected for practical reasons. The color was believed to reduce eye strain for operators who spent hours staring at screens and monitors.

Historical Context

In the 1960s and 1970s, control rooms were designed with this muted green hue because:

  • It was believed to be calming and reduce stress
  • The color complemented early monochrome displays
  • It was easy to maintain and clean
  • It was the standard in industrial design at the time

Modern Control Rooms

Today's control rooms have moved away from the seafoam green aesthetic, opting for more neutral grays and blues. But the legacy of this design choice lives on in retro tech aesthetics.

The Nostalgia Factor

Many tech enthusiasts now appreciate the vintage look of these control rooms. The seafoam green has become a symbol of an era when computing was just beginning to change the world.
